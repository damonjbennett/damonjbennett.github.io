Rory McIlroy’s Major slump is more impressive than some entire PGA Tour careers.

golf
pgatour

Author

Damon Bennett

Published

May 4, 2025

3,900.

That’s the number of days between Rory McIlroy’s fourth Major win and his fifth. For more than 10 years, McIlroy continued his hunt for the legendary career Grand Slam, a feat only five golfers had accomplished before he defeated Justin Rose in a single-hole playoff at the 2025 Masters.

An uninformed viewer could say that after such a hot start to his career (four major victories in four years) that McIlroy didn’t live up to expectations for the next decade.

That uninformed viewer needs to stick to the local municipal course.

In the 10 years following McIlroy’s one-stroke victory (notice a pattern?) in the 2014 PGA Championship, only one player collected more prize money than him: Scottie Scheffler, who won two Masters tournaments in 2022 and 2024.

McIlroy was still winning tournaments, just not the Majors. The ending of the 2024 U.S. Open probably still haunts him, but his performance outside of Major tournaments was the best in the entire PGA Tour.

McIlroy is well-known for his consistent tee shots. The 5’9” Northern Irishman can swing his driver like very few, and his average driving distance of 320 yards in 2024 was second-best on the PGA Tour.

However, driving distance is far from a success indicator on the PGA Tour. Cameron Champ, who boasted the longest average drive of 322 yards, played three events and finished inside the top-25 only once.

Mcilroy combines driving distance with striking accuracy, and as of May 4, he ranks first in strokes gained off the tee, and total strokes gained, statistics that measure how a specific part of a hole (tee shot, approach, putting) impacts a player’s score.

From 2015 to 2024, 16 players on the PGA Tour gained strokes off the tee in at least 8 seasons. Only three of those players ever beat McIlroy at the tee box: Luke List and Gary Woodland in 2018, and Jhonattan Vegas in 2020.

Rory McIlroy is one of the best golfers of all time. He proved this not only by joining one of the most exclusive clubs in the world as a career Grand Slam winner, but by spending 10 years proving that playing at the highest level does not always translate to winning at the biggest stage.

So next time your friends ask why your 8 handicap suddenly becomes 20 when they’re around, show them this.
